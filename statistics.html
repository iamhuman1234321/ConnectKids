The Opportunity Gap

Scale

  • Millions of children are poor or low-income in the U.S. (~36–37 million people in poverty, 2023–2024 Census). Child poverty sharply affects those most likely to lack extracurricular access. (Census.gov)
  • ~15 million low-income children lacked summer programs in 2023; 24.6 million families report being unable to access afterschool programs, a 60% increase since 2004. (Gallup.com, Afterschool Alliance)
  • Participation gaps persist: structured summer programs are ~2x more common among higher-income families (27% vs 14% in 2019). (Wallace Foundation)
  • Extracurricular participation improves engagement, college attendance, and long-term outcomes. Each child with consistent access gains measurable lifelong benefits. (NCES)

Neglectedness

  • Cost and accessibility are primary barriers. Many families wish to enroll children but cannot due to price, transport, or awareness. (Afterschool Alliance)
  • Program capacity lags: nearly 3 in 4 families lack access in many areas. Unmet demand remains painfully high. (Afterschool Alliance)
  • Policy and funding fluctuations create episodic gaps; tech and nonprofit solutions can scale quickly to fill them. (TIME)
  • Takeaway: problem is both large (tens of millions affected) and under-resourced — strong argument for a focused solution.
